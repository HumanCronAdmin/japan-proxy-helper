Important Change (Aug 29, 2025): The US eliminated the $800 duty-free de minimis threshold for shipments from China and Hong Kong. While Japan shipments are not directly affected, some carriers have updated procedures.

What Changed

  • The $800 de minimis exemption was removed for packages from China/HK as of August 29, 2025.
  • Japan Post temporarily suspended US-bound parcel service due to regulatory uncertainty.
  • Packages from Japan via private carriers (DHL, FedEx) continue to clear normally.

How Each Proxy Handles It

  • DHL/FedEx shippers (Buyee, White Rabbit Express): Handle customs declarations automatically. Duties invoiced to you if applicable.
  • EMS/SAL shippers: May experience delays at US customs. Accurate value declaration is critical.
  • Tenso: You choose the carrier — DHL/FedEx recommended for US destinations.
Tips for US Buyers:
  • Choose DHL or FedEx over EMS for faster, more predictable US customs clearance.
  • Always declare accurate item values — undervaluing risks seizure.
  • Budget an extra 5-15% for potential duties on items over $800.
  • Keep purchase receipts for customs verification if requested.
  • Consolidate packages when possible to reduce per-shipment handling fees.
